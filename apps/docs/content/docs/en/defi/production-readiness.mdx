---
title: Production readiness
description:
  Operational checklist for financial applications on Solana - transaction
  landing, RPC strategy, risk limits, and monitoring.
---

Financial applications fail differently from consumer apps: the cost of a bug is
bounded only by your balance sheet. Before going live, work through this
checklist.

## Transaction landing

- Priority-fee strategy driven by live fee-market data, not constants:
  [add priority fees](/developers/cookbook/transactions/add-priority-fees)
- Compute budgets measured and set per transaction type:
  [optimize compute](/developers/cookbook/transactions/optimize-compute)
- Blockhash expiry and retry policy that can't double-execute - understand
  [confirmation & expiration](/developers/cookbook/transactions/confirmation)
  and [retries](/developers/cookbook/transactions/retry); use
  [durable nonces](/developers/cookbook/transactions/durable-nonces) where an
  approval gap separates signing from sending
- [MEV protection](/docs/defi/mev-protection) on intent-revealing flows;
  [stake-weighted QoS](/docs/defi/stake-weighted-qos) for sustained
  latency-sensitive volume

## RPC strategy

- At least two providers with health-checked failover
- Websocket reconnect logic that resubscribes and back-fills missed slots
- Rate limits understood and load-tested at 10x expected volume

## Risk controls

- Per-transaction, per-strategy, and global position limits enforced in code
- Slippage bounds on every swap; simulation before send with divergence alerts
- Kill switch that flattens or halts - tested, and reachable by a human at 3am

## Monitoring

- Fill rates, landing latency, and failure rates by type
  ([index program activity](/docs/defi/index-program-activity))
- Balance reconciliation between expected and onchain state every slot batch
- Alerting on oracle staleness for every feed you depend on
  ([market data](/docs/defi/market-data))

## Security

- Hot wallets hold working capital only; withdrawal paths to cold storage
- Keys in an HSM/KMS or signer service - see [Keychain](/docs/tools/keychain)
- Third-party program dependencies pinned and monitored for upgrades
