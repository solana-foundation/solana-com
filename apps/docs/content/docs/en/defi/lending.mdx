  Integrate Solana lending markets — deposits and yield, borrowing against

Lending markets are the money layer of Solana DeFi: they set the cost of
leverage and provide the yield floor. The major venues —
[Kamino](https://docs.kamino.finance), [marginfi](https://docs.marginfi.com),
and [Save (formerly Solend)](https://docs.save.finance) — expose similar
primitives with venue-specific SDKs.

## The shared model

- **Deposit** an asset into a reserve/bank and receive an interest-accruing
  claim on it.
- **Borrow** against deposited collateral up to a loan-to-value limit, at a
  utilization-driven rate.
- **Health factor** summarizes a position's distance from liquidation; when it
  crosses the threshold, liquidators repay debt in exchange for discounted
  collateral.

Prices come from [oracles](/docs/defi/market-data) — every integration inherits
oracle risk, so understand which feeds a market uses.

## Integration notes

- Treat venue SDKs as the source of truth for account layouts; they change. Pin
  versions and monitor migrations.
- Deposits/borrows compose with other instructions in
  [atomic transactions](/docs/defi/compose-transactions) — e.g. deposit and
  borrow in a single transaction.
- If you build liquidation or monitoring infrastructure, you need real-time
  position data: [Index program activity](/docs/defi/index-program-activity) and
  low-latency [transaction landing](/docs/defi/production-readiness).

## Risk checklist before integrating a market

- Oracle sources and staleness behavior under congestion
- Interest-rate model behavior at 100% utilization (can withdrawals halt?)
- Admin keys and upgrade authority on the program
- Historical incident record and audits
