---
title: Keep transfer amounts private
description:
  Use confidential transfers to encrypt token balances and transfer amounts
  while keeping addresses auditable.
---

Institutional flows often can't reveal position sizes or payment amounts to the
public — treasury operations, payroll, and fund subscriptions all leak strategy
if amounts are visible. The
[confidential transfer extension](/docs/tokens/extensions/confidential-transfer)
encrypts token account balances and transfer amounts using homomorphic
encryption and zero-knowledge proofs, while the accounts and the fact that a
transfer happened remain public.

## What is and isn't hidden

| Visible onchain                | Encrypted                               |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Sender and recipient addresses | Transfer amount                         |
| Mint and token type            | Account balances (confidential portion) |
| Timing of transfers            | —                                       |

An optional **auditor key** can be configured at mint creation, letting a
designated party (the issuer or a regulator) decrypt amounts without making them
public — usually the deciding factor for compliance sign-off.

## Design notes

- Confidential transfers are opt-in per token account; accounts can hold both a
  public and a confidential balance for the same mint.
- Combine with [Token ACL](/docs/tokenization/token-acl) when you need both
  holder restrictions and amount privacy.
- Indexers see transfer events without amounts — plan reporting around the
  auditor key. See
  [Index token activity](/docs/tokenization/index-token-activity).
