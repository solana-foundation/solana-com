---
title: Production readiness
description:
  Operational checklist for running a tokenized asset in production - key
  management, compliance runbooks, monitoring, and incident response.
---

A tokenized asset is an operational commitment, not a deployment. Before mainnet
launch, work through this checklist.

## Key management

- Every authority (mint, freeze, pause, permanent delegate, metadata update, ACL
  admin) is held by a multisig or program-controlled PDA - never a single hot
  key.
- Signing ceremonies are documented: who approves a mint, who can freeze, what
  quorum pauses the token.
- Key rotation is tested. You have rehearsed
  [set authority](/docs/tokens/basics/set-authority) transfers on devnet.

## Compliance runbooks

Write and rehearse the response before you need it:

- **Sanctions hit** - freeze the account, document the action, notify counsel.
  Target time from alert to freeze.
- **Incident pause** - criteria for pausing the whole token, who decides, and
  the communication plan for holders.
- **Seizure order** - if you enabled a
  [permanent delegate](/docs/tokens/extensions/permanent-delegate), the legal
  review and execution path for using it.

## Monitoring and reconciliation

- Supply vs reserves reconciled continuously -
  [index token activity](/docs/tokenization/index-token-activity) from day one.
- Alerts on: large transfers, mint/burn outside the issuance pipeline, authority
  changes, freeze events.
- A public status/attestation page if your asset is marketed as fully reserved.

## Technical operations

- Redundant RPC providers; don't run compliance actions through a single point
  of failure.
- Transaction landing strategy for urgent actions (freezes must land during
  congestion) - see
  [retrying transactions](/developers/cookbook/transactions/retry) and
  [priority fees](/developers/cookbook/transactions/add-priority-fees).
- Devnet mirror of the full configuration for rehearsals and upgrades.
