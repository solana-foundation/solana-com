May brought new highs across Solana’s RWA ecosystem, tokenized equities activity, stablecoin supply, perps volume, and spot ETF inflows. Solana’s share of cumulative onchain tokenized equities spot trading volume reached 97%, [RWA total value](https://app.rwa.xyz/networks/solana) reached a new all-time high of $2.8B+, and spot Solana ETF AUM crossed $1B. The month also brought the first stablecoin issued by a U.S. national bank available directly within a banking app. A global payments network activated Solana-native stablecoin infrastructure.

![](/uploads/posts/solana-ecosystem-roundup-may-2026/97.jpeg)

The ecosystem additions were broad: new validator clients, a redesigned token program, new rate market infrastructure, expanded agent tooling, and RWA asset classes that included reinsurance, spirits, EV hardware, energy infrastructure, and physical silver alongside equities and stablecoins.

## May by the Numbers

- $2.8B+ in RWA value: Solana’s RWA ecosystem reached a new all-time high during May, with new highs across value, holders, active addresses, and transfer volume
- 97% tokenized equities share: Solana accounted for 97% of cumulative onchain tokenized equities spot trading volume
- $16.4B stablecoin supply: Solana stablecoin supply crossed $16B during May
- $64.6B in perps volume: Solana perps venues reached a new monthly volume [record in May](https://defillama.com/perps/chain/Solana?groupBy=monthly)
- $115.3M in ETF inflows: US spot Solana ETFs recorded highest net inflows in May, with zero outflow days

## Infrastructure

At the protocol and developer layer, May brought upgrades aimed at making Solana cheaper, easier to build on, and more resilient for high-throughput applications.

Anza shipped P-Token to mainnet, an optimized rewrite of the SPL Token program that reduces token instruction compute costs by approximately 96%, freeing 12–13% more block space for the broader network without changing block limits.

Jito [introduced](https://bam.dev/blog/introducing-firebam-bam-expands-to-firedancer/) FireBAM, a Frankendancer-compatible validator client merging Firedancer's performance and throughput with BAM's privacy and deterministic execution via trusted execution environments. FireBAM supports a transition path to full Firedancer when it becomes available.

Solana joined the Open Transaction Layer as a founding partner to develop cross-chain standards for identity, compliance, and settlement messaging.

## A Broader Surface Area

May's breadth was notable even by recent standards. The same month that delivered bank-issued stablecoins and a European UCITS fund expansion also saw validator clients merge, a core token program ship its first major rewrite, and the agentic payment layer gain enough MCP tooling to start looking like infrastructure rather than experiment.

The through-line is not a single theme but a pattern: more of the institutions, issuers, and developers that have historically watched from the sidelines are now building on or integrating with Solana directly. The RWA all-time high, the tokenized equities volume share, and the strongest month of ETF inflows this year all reflect the same underlying shift. The network's production footprint keeps expanding.
