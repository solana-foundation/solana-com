## **SK Hynix: The memory supercycle hits Nasdaq**

[SK Hynix](https://www.skhynix.com/) has been publicly traded in Korea for decades, but its American Depositary Receipt (ADR) debut today is historic. The company is raising approximately **$28 billion**, marking the [largest stock issuance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/sk-hynixs-28-billion-nasdaq-162700620.html) from a foreign company in U.S. markets. Proceeds from this offering will fund the Yongin “Y1” mega-fab and next-gen HBM4 production lines at a time when memory bandwidth is the primary bottleneck in AI infrastructure.

**What SK Hynix Builds**

SK Hynix builds three kinds of memory:

- **DRAM,** which is the fast memory found in computers, phones, and servers tasked with holding temporary data for active applications;
- **NAND flash,** which is the hardware inside Solid State Drives (SSDs), phones, and memory cards that keeps data intact even when the power is off, and most critically;
- **High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM),** which is a specialized, ultra-premium variant of DRAM where memory chips are stacked vertically like skyscrapers and linked by microscopic wires. 

**Why HBM Matters for AI**\
Traditional DRAM lays chips flat across a board, requiring data to travel comparatively long horizontal distances between memory and the processor. HBM, however, stacks chips vertically like floors in a skyscraper and connects each layer through microscopic wires, allowing for memory to be placed in stacks mere millimeters from the GPU. The result: up to 10x higher bandwidth, exactly what large language models and AI training demand.

**The Supercycle is Here**

Demand for HBM has ignited a semiconductor supercycle. SK Hynix commands:

- **Approximately 59%** share of the global HBM market.
- **Roughly 70%** of Nvidia’s HBM4 allocation under contract.
- A fully sold-out 2026 HBM production pipeline

Nvidia now accounts for **about 27% of SK Hynix revenue**. In June 2026, SK Hynix overtook Samsung to become South Korea’s most valuable listed company.
