As a Solana Developer Platform launch partner, Crossmint helps companies embed smart wallets, move fiat into stablecoins, sponsor transactions, and connect onchain settlement to real-world payment rails through a single API. Customers including Western Union, fomo, and Wirex show how Solana-based financial products can reach production without requiring every enterprise team to build blockchain infrastructure from scratch.

## The Problem: enterprises want to build on Solana, but the path to production is fragmented

Sub-second finality, sub-cent fees, and Token Extensions have answered "is Solana ready?" For enterprise payment products, those properties matter because high-frequency transfers, small-value payments, gas-sponsored wallets, and compliance-aware token flows only work if settlement is fast, inexpensive, and programmable. The harder question is how to ship fast and compliantly.

Historically, that required stitching together five or more vendors: embedded wallets, onramps, offramps, stablecoin orchestration, KYC/KYB and Travel Rule compliance, gas sponsorship, and transaction monitoring. Each integration meant a new API, contract, and failure point.

> “Enterprises come to Solana with a clear vision but no clear path to handling money movement. That's the gap we fill.”
>
> — Rodrigo Fernández Touza, Co-Founder of Crossmint

## Emerging Capability: Agentic finance

While the customer examples above are live enterprise payment and wallet deployments, Crossmint is also extending the same smart-wallet model to AI agents. 

AI agents are becoming economic actors. They’re subscribing to APIs, paying for compute, and increasingly have been transacting on behalf of users. For agents to operate autonomously, the wallet itself has to enforce the rules. EOAs can't: spending limits live in application code, and a bug in the agent bypasses them. Crossmint's [smart contract wallets](https://docs.crossmint.com/wallets/why-smart-wallets?utm_source=solana&utm_medium=organic) on Solana solve this with a two-signer architecture: an owner signer with full control, and an agent signer (sealed in a TEE) with scoped permissions enforced by the wallet itself. The agent decides when to pay. The smart contract enforces how much.
