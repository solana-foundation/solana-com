## The Problem: Deploying stablecoin rails is complex, and requires stitching together multiple vendors

Solana settles in under a second, for a fraction of a cent. Token Extensions add token-level controls like confidential transfers and programmable compliance. Those capabilities make it an ideal settlement layer for enterprises deploying stablecoin embedded finance and money movement products. Getting a product to market is the harder part.

Deploying stablecoin rails normally requires stitching together five or more vendors: embedded wallets, onramps, offramps, stablecoin orchestration, KYC/KYB and Travel Rule compliance, gas sponsorship, and transaction monitoring. Each integration means a new API, a new contract, and a new failure point. Integrating them also demands significant in-house blockchain, payments, and regulatory expertise.

\
As Rodrigo Fernández Touza, Co-Founder of Crossmint, put it:&#x20;

> "Enterprises come to Solana with a vision of leveraging stablecoins for faster, cheaper payment infrastructure. What they lack is a clear path to production. That's the gap we fill."

As a regulated entity across the markets it serves, Crossmint can act as the licensed layer onboarding and servicing end users, so builders ship a compliant product without assembling and maintaining the pieces themselves.

## Top use cases

Across fintech, enterprises are deploying stablecoin rails on Solana for three core use cases:

**Embedded finance.** Enterprises offer stablecoin financial services inside their own apps. Users get a wallet and a full set of financial products on top of it: fund with Apple/Google Pay, cards, or transfers, hold stablecoins and earn yield on balances, spend at any merchant with a Visa-powered card, and cash out to local fiat, all without touching blockchain complexity.

**Money movement.** Businesses hold, convert, and move value on licensed rails. Treasury wallets custody funds, on/offramps handle conversion, and stablecoin payouts reach 160+ countries, so payroll, remittance, and B2B settlement flows run without a patchwork of separately licensed vendors.

**Agentic payments.** AI agents transact within limits the enterprise controls. Policy-bound wallets and cards let agents pay autonomously while the wallet itself enforces spending rules, with human oversight and full audit trails on every transaction.

## Emerging: agentic finance

The same infrastructure is now extending to a new kind of user: AI agents. Agents are becoming economic actors, subscribing to APIs, paying for compute, and transacting on behalf of users. To transact securely, compliantly, and within limits, agents also need a payment stack.

Crossmint provides that stack on Solana: onramps and treasury for machine-to-machine payments, cards and stablecoins for consumer and B2B transactions, agent checkouts and catalogs to buy from, and the guardrails and risk controls that keep every transaction inside the limits an individual or business sets.
